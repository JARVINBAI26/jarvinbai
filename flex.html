self respect movement

The Self-Respect Movement is a South Asian movement with the aim of achieving a society in which backward castes have equal human rights, [1] and encouraging backward castes to have self-respect in the context of a caste-based society that considered them to be a lower end of the hierarchy.

It was founded in 1925 by S. Ramanathan[3] who invited E. V. Ramasamy (also known as Periyar by his followers) to head the movement in Tamil Nadu India against Brahminism. The movement was extremely influential not just in Tamil Nadu, but also overseas in countries with large Tamil populations, such as Malaysia and Singapore.

Among Singapore Indians, groups like the Tamil Reform Association, and leaders such as Thamizhavel G. Sarangapani were prominent in promoting the principles of the Self-Respect Movement among the local Tamil population through schools and publications
